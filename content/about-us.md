### Our Story

**It started with a simple observation: teams today use too many tools.**

We were building software for a living, and we were terrible at managing our own work. We had tasks in Jira. Files in Google Drive. Time tracking in Harvest. HR in BambooHR. Client communication scattered across email and Slack. Billing in a separate accounting system.

Every morning started with the same ritual: open five apps, check five different dashboards, mentally reconcile five different versions of reality. Every month ended with the same scramble: export data from three systems, reconcile in Excel, generate reports, wonder why the numbers didn't match.

We weren't alone. Every organization we spoke to was running 5–10 different SaaS products to manage their operations. The names changed — Asana instead of Jira, Dropbox instead of Drive, Toggl instead of Harvest — but the pattern was always the same. Fragmented tools. Fragmented data. Fragmented teams.

**The cost was staggering:**

- **Productivity loss** — The average knowledge worker switches between tools 37 times per day. Each switch costs focus.
- **Data fragmentation** — Project health lived in one tool. Financial health in another. People data in a third. No single source of truth.
- **Rising costs** — A 50-person team easily spends $4,000+/month across 6+ SaaS tools. And that cost grows with every hire.
- **Security exposure** — Managing passwords, permissions, and access across a dozen platforms is impossible to do consistently.

We knew there had to be a better way.

**So we built it.**

MyWorkSpace was designed from the ground up as a single, unified platform where every dimension of business operations — people, projects, files, time, clients, and finances — lives in one system. Not integrated. Not synced. One system.

No integrations to maintain. No data to export and re-import. No context-switching between tabs.

Just one platform that works the way your team works.

**The name says it all:** MyWorkSpace is where your work lives. Not a launcher for other tools. Not a dashboard that pulls from other systems. The actual workspace where work gets done.
